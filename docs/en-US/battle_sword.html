SWORD COMBAT


When controlling Arche, combat works somewhat like a tournament fighter, with sword combos and martial arts moves being used to defeat enemies. Simply swinging your sword blindly will not ensure victory; you must use your techniques properly to dominate your foe, especially on harder difficulties.

Each sword technique has its own strengths and weaknesses; learn to use the right technique based on enemy position, distance, etc.

Basic Techniques & Button Combinations
Slash The most basic attack.
Thrust Weaker than a slash, but faster.
Wide Slash Longer and stronger than a normal slash, but slow and leaves you open.
Arcing Slash Useful against aerial enemies.
Snap Thrust Allows you to quickly attack enemies behind you.

If You Can't Get Your Moves On
There's nothing wrong with a bit of practice! If you're having trouble executing a particular move, return to a town or other safe area and practice there. You won't hurt anyone in a town.

Complex Techniques
There are far more techniques than what are listed here. You will learn complex combat maneuvers, evasive abilities, and more as you progress through the game and level up. These can be viewed under the Abilities menu in the main menu. (A list can also be found here.)
Create Your Own Fighting Style!

The huge majority of sword techniques are simple moves by themselves. Players are encouraged, however, to weave these moves together to create their own fighting style. As your level grows, so too will you technique list and your possibilities.

Examples:
Three-Hit Combo A Thrust, Slash and Wide Slash form the basic combo.
The Pincushion A quick succession of Thrusts and Snap Thrusts in the opposite direction allow you to attack foes from both directions.
The Backstab A Roll chained into a Snap Thrust allow you to roll "through" an enemy and assault them from the rear!
Mach Slash Chain a Thrust, a Slash and a Combo Slash together for the fastest possible three-attack combo.
Boot To The Head You can use a Jumpkick followed by a Cartwheel to attack a foe and dodge away and then draw your sword, ready for battle!
The Yo-yo Alternate between Wheel Slashes and Twirling Slashes to create a spinning vortex of steel!
The Full Moon Descends Open with The Full Moon to knock a foe from the air, and then use the Downstab to nail them to the floor and finish them.

Technique Settings
In the Abilities menu, you can configure whether or not certain techiques will be used. All are enabled by default, but if one is disabled, you cannot execute it even if you input the command (which can be handy if you do not wish to use a particular move by accident).
