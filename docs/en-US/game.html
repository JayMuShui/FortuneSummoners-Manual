HOW TO PLAY


Fortune Summoners is a real-time RPG in which your characters adventure together in a party.

You will discover what you must do from your interactions with others in towns and other safe areas. Once you do, ensure you have what you need from the local shops, gather your party, and venture forth into the wilderness!


The UI

① Controlled Character Info Panel Controlled character's HP, MP, Level, and EXP. Stars indicate remaining levels until cap.
② Currently Selected Magic Currently selected magic. Not displayed if the controlled character is not a magician.
③ Allies' Status Panel Controlled character and allies' HP (Yellow) and MP (Blue). Not displayed when alone.
④ Currency Held Display Current money on hand.
⑤ Shortcut Bar Shortcut listing (F1 through F6).
⑥ Current Target Enemy Info Current target enemy's HP. Not displayed when not under attack.
⑦ Combo Meter Combo Meter. Displays added damage from combinations. Displayed whenever entering combat.
⑧ Status/Magic Effect Panel Negative status effects and magic effects will have their symbol displayed here when you are afflicted by them.
Towns and villages
Towns and villages are where you will learn more of your quest and your current objectives.


Press X (with your weapon sheathed if you are controlling Arche) while near someone to speak to them. You can also press X in front of objects like chests and drawers to investigate them.



Towns have shops as well. You can purchase essential equipment and items in these shops, and sell anything you don't need.



Not every character can use every weapon or piece of armor, so choose carefully and pay attention to who can equip what. You may equip or use items purchased by pressing F1 and selecting "Items".



If you stay at an inn, you can restore your HP and MP for a small fee. You may also save at inns.



If you change characters...
People in towns can react differently depending on who is in front of the party (that is, who the player controls) at any given time. Once some friends join your party, try talking to people with multiple characters. What you can do with bookshelves and the like can change depending on your lead character as well.




Using the Menu
Press F1 to open the main menu. From this menu, you can view and use items, check the status and abilities of your characters, read travel notes to remind yourself of what your current quest is, change options, and exit the game.

Items Here you may use, equip/de-equip, and discard any possessions. You may also cycle between item sorting modes (between "type" and "order obtained") by pressing Z.
Special Items Here you may view items that are essential to your quest. These cannot be sold or discarded.
Status This option allows you to view detailed information about your party members.
Abilities Here you may view and configure the usage of a character's spells or special attacks.
Party Commands You may alter how your AI companions behave in battle here.
Messages This option allows you to read notes and messages from your party members and others concerning your quests. If there are unread messages, you will see a small envelope icon.
You can learn more about lettershere.
System Here you may alter game options or quit the game.
Fighting Monsters
Outside of towns, you will be accosted by the foul beasts of the land regularly. The music will change when you enter into combat. Use your sword and magic to emerge victorious!
For more details, see "Combat Basics".


The Spoils of Battle
Defeating monsters will reward all characters present with experience points (EXP). Enemies will also drop coins and, occasionally, treasure chests. Coins and items can be picked up simply by touching them.
Copper Copper coins are the basic coinage of Scotsholm.
Silver Silver coins are worth ten copper.
Gold Gold coins are worth a hundred copper.
Chests Chests contain a variety of useful items, depending on the monster which dropped it.

There is a limit to how much money you can carry at any one time, dependant on how many Marks of Heroism you have found; once you reach this limit, you cannot pick up any more coins. Please see "Levels and Attributes" for more information.
Traps, Puzzles and Obstacles
In your adventures, you will encounter not just monsters but cliffs, water, traps, puzzles, and other obstacles.


Cliffs and water can simply be jumped over. If you fall into a pit or into water, you will lose a little HP and be returned to the point from which you fell. Jumping is triggered with the C key by default, and the longer the button is held, the further you jump (to an extent). You can use the left and right arrow keys to move while in the air.

If you find a switch or something else to investigate, press X (with your sword sheathed if controlling Arche) to investigate. This means you cannot investigate things when monsters are nearby, however.

AI-controlled characters cannot follow along on moving platforms, but if the player character reaches a room exit, your teammates will follow.





Special Abilities
Each character possesses abilities that they alone can utilize. Using these effectively will allow you to reach places you otherwise cannot. To use them, take control of the character in question.

Arche can use her incredible girlstength to push blocks, statues and other objects that Sana and Stella would find immovable. To do this, sheathe your sword and press against the object in question.
Sana's elemental stone allows her to breathe underwater for indefinite periods of time, even without the aid of the Mermaid Robe spell. Simply have Sana enter water to activate the effect. Note that this does NOT protect her from extreme temperatures or monsters in the water!
Stella can use her Fire Handling ability to burn away brambles and other obstacles.


Meeting An Untimely End
Should all of your party members be reduced to 0 HP from either monster attacks or harmful obstacles, your party will... take a long nap, as pictured. You will be given two options in this case:

Try Again You will restart from the room previous to the one you were defeated in, with your HP, MP, and items in the state they were before you proceeded forward.
Return to Safety Returns you to the last town you visited, with your HP and MP restored, but any items you consumed during your defeat still consumed and you will lose a little money. This option may not always be available.

If you somehow find yourself in an impossible situation in your travels, you may use the "Give Up" option to immediately game over and have the option to return to a town.

Note that "safety" counts as towns and villages, and does not include save points in dungeons.


Saving and Quitting
Your game can be saved at inns, at the school nurse's office, and in select areas inside certain dungeons and out in the countryside.

To quit the game, select the "Return to Title" option from the System menu in the main menu. This does not save the game, however, so make sure to save beforehand.
