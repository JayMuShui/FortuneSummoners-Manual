EXPERIENCE AND LEVELS


Character Growth

When your party defeats monsters or other foes, all party members present (and on their feet) receive experience points. Once you gain enough points, you will gain a level, increasing your attributes and possibly giving you a new spell or ability.

Attribute and ability/spell gains will be displayed when you press F1 after a level up.

There is, however, a limit to the number of levels you can gain. Should you reach your current level cap (which begins at 3), you cannot gain any experience.

To increase your level cap, you must obtain Marks of Heroism, like the one shown here.

The level cap is party-wide; even members not in the party will benefit from Marks gained when they are not in the party. Marks also increase the amount of money you can carry.


Character Attributes
You can check a character's attributes from the Status screen in the main menu.

Level Displays a character's current and maximum possible character level.
HP Displays a character's current and maximum HP.
MP Displays a character's current and maximum MP.
Status The character's status. Can be normal/poisoned/exploded/etc.

Attack Determines attack power with physical attacks, such as sword attacks. Displays combined value (with equipped items) and base value.
Defense Determines the damage taken from physical attacks. Displays combined value (with equipped items) and base value.
Spirit Determines the power of both healing and attack spells. Displays combined value (with equipped items) and base value.
Resist Determines the damage taken from magic, and how likely a character is to resist status effects. Displays combined value (with equipped items) and base value.

Adventurer Level Adventuerer Level is determined by the number of Marks of Heroism you have discovered.
Combat Level Combat Level is a display of how close you are to the current level cap.
EXP Displays the amount of experience you've gained in your current level.
EXP to level Displays the amount of EXP you need for the next level.

Weapon The character's currently equipped weapon.
Armor #1 The character's currently equipped body armor.
Armor #2 The character's currently equipped secondary armor, such as gloves or a cloak.
Accessory The character's currently equipped decorative accessory.

Marks Gained A tally of how many of the game's total Marks of Heroism you've gained.
Monsters Defeated A tally of how many monsters you have defeated.
Money Gained A tally of how much money you've found in the course of your quest.
EXP gained A running total of how much EXP you've gained over the course of your adventure.
Total Party Deaths The number of times your party has been wiped out.
Longest Combo A tally of the longest number of consective hits delivered in one combo.
Max Damage Dealt Most damage dealt to an enemy. Displays seperate records for physical attacks and magical attacks.


Adventurer Level and Combat Level
A character's total level is determined by a combination of their adventurer level and combat level.

When you collect a Mark of Heroism, your adventurer level increases and your combat level decreases; this effectively works as a simple increase in your level cap. If, however, you collect a Mark when your combat level is 0, you will gain a character level as combat level cannot be reduced any further.

In the prologue, Marks only increase combat level as opposed to adventurer level and effectively act as a simple level cap expander.
