ARCHE'S ADVICE

Collect Info!
As the story progresses, people can say new stuff, so try talking to them lots, not just once!

Also! There's places you can visit that aren't related to the story at all. I bet there's lots of neat stuff waiting there!
Work Together To Solve Mysteries!
Dungeons have lots of puzzles and stuff, not just monsters, and a lot of them can't be solved with just one person! You'll need to change your Follow Mode and stuff to get everyone in just the right place to figure it out.

If you forgot how Follow Modes work, look here, okay?
Make Sure To Use Magic Right!
When you're in dungeons and stuff, if you don't conserve your magic, you'll totally run out of MP really fast! You'll run out even if you have four tens of MP! And that's terrible! You need to tell your mage allies to lay off the magic!

Of course, if you're up against a big nasty boss monster or something, why hold back?! Have your mages go nuts! If your mages hold back, you won't get stuff like support spells, and those can really help!

If you don't remember how to change magic use modes, look here!
Guarding is Important in Combat!
If you just swing your sword like a crazy person, all you'll manage to do is get beat up! You have to guard against or avoid an enemy's attack, then counter! You can turn on Auto-guard if you want the game to help you with that, and you can use it to learn an enemy's attack pattern before beating them into tar!

A lot of enemies will do stuff before they attack, like slimes quivering, so learn to look for that, too!
Don't Forget To Level Up!
If you're finding enemies tough, or bosses impossible, check your current level! If you're, like, more than two levels below your current max level? Try leveling up some! If you ARE at max level, look for any Marks of Heroism you might've missed. I think there's a fortune-teller somewhere who can tell you if you missed any...

Oh, if you want to know about levels, look here!
Choose The Right Difficulty Level!
Your experience gain - and how hard you get punched in the face - changes depending on the difficulty level you selected. Once you've gotten used to the controls, and if you want to level up really fast, you can try increasing the difficulty. If you find a certain boss REALLY hard, you can turn it down, too.

Oh yeah, and in the prologue, the experience curve isn't very high, so changing difficulties won't do much.

(You can change difficulty in the Options section of the main menu!)
Heal Early, Heal Often!
Try using healing items as soon as my HP drops to half or below, okay? AI characters will heal at about that at default, but you might want to help them out with items if two or three people are low on health all at once.
