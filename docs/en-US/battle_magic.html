MAGICAL COMBAT


When controlling Sana or Stella, you will support your allies with a host of magical spells.


Magic comes in several varieties, including direct damage spells which assault your enemy, indirect attack spells which do things like put foes to sleep, healing magic to soothe injured teammates, and support magic which provides allies with various benefits. As you gain levels, your mages will learn a wider variety of spells (a full list can be found here.)
Using Magic

While magic is powerful, using it will consume your MP. If you lack the MP to cast a spell, it will fizzle, so take care to preserve your MP for when you need it.

1:Select A Spell Z is the default key for selecting a spell.
You may press 1 through 0 on the number row or pad as well.

If you possess multiple magic books, you may switch between them with F5.
2: Casting Hold the attack button (default X) until the magic circle is complete.
You may press Z to cancel a spell which is ready to cast.
3: Unleashing The Fury Release X to unleash the spell.

Using Magic On Allies
Healing magic and other beneficial spells will automatically target the person which the game believes will benefit the most from a given spell, but you can press up or down to change the target of the spell while casting.

You will not be able to target people who are too far away from you, however. Typically this means they're completely across, or off, the screen from you.

Basic Magical Attacks
Magic users are also able to use simple element-shaping spells or even their simple defensive weapons to attack their foes in a way that does not require MP usage.
Basic Attack Aggrieve a foe with your staff or dagger. Not as strong as a sword swing.
Back Attack Snap around and swing your weapon behind you.
Magical Shot Shoot water or fire at a foe.
Anti-Air Magical Shot Shoot your element above your head at airborne foes.
Long-Range Magical Shot Fires a longer-ranged magical attack. Leaves the caster open for a moment.
Leap Back Jump backward to dodge an attack.

Magic Books
In the abilities menu you can assign spells you have learned to one of three different books (A, B, or C). Newly-learned spells will be assigned to a book automatically when learned, so dealing with your spellbook is not necessary early in the game. Later in the game, however, you will want to manage your spellbooks for easy access to handy spells.

Up to ten spells can be assigned to one book; spells not assigned to at least one book cannot be used. Pressing F5 will change the currently equipped spellbook. Spells placed in spellbook C are still available to the player, but will not be used by the AI.

The recommended set-up is to place commonly used spells in book A, buffs and other uncommon spells in B, and spells you only wish to use directly in book C.

Using Magic Effectively
It's well worth remember that, aside from a few examples of direct level upgrades to certain spells (such as Heal Water Level 2), every single spell behaves differently; they are not simple numerical upgrades to one another. It's worth your time to try out spells in a safe environment to see what they can do, so you can determine how best to use them in a dangerous situation later on.
