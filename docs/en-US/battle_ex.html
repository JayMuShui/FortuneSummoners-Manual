ADVANCED COMBAT


While individual moves are important in Fortune Summoners, the real core of the game is working with your party to overcome trials.

Full understanding of how to give the AI orders, controlling the camera, status effects, unblockable attacks and combos will give you an edge in battle.
About Combos
If you land five or more hits on an enemy, the combo meter will display on the left-hand side of the screen, and if you continue the combo attack, the meter will continue to fill and you will deal more damage to an enemy. At 50% you deal 1.5 times normal damage; by 200%, the maximum, your damage is tripled.

If there are multiple foes on screen, hitting any of them will extend the combo. Attacks from all your allies are counted toward the combo; if anyone hits anything, the combo is continued. You can cooperate with your party members to pull off massive combos - and massive damage.

Finally, changing the element of an attack causes the gauge to rise faster (i.e. a sword attack followed by a water attack followed by a fire attack.)
Status Effects
Monsters and traps can sometimes inflict status effects on your party members. Some of your spells and special weapons can do likewise to your foes.

Sleep
Sleeping characters cannot act, and are incapable of defending themselves at all, menaing they take extra damage from attacks. Being attacked will help you wake up, though!
Poison
Poisoned characters will take steady damage as time goes on. Poison can be cured with items, spells, or by returning to a safe location such as a town.
Paralysis/Freeze
Characters who are paralyzed or frozen solid are incapable of moving at all. This effect fades naturally after a brief amount of time.
Confusion
Confused character will attack their allies as well as their enemies! Characters in this state cannot be directly controlled. This effect will fade after some time passes (or if you beat the crud out of your friend).
Disabled
While the lives of our young protagonists do not end when they hit 0 HP, they will be knocked to the ground during a fight, and afterward, they will be too injured and exhausted to contribute to further fights. Special restorative items can return some of their vigor, and returning to a town also gives them a tiny bit of HP.
Unblockable Attacks

While most attacks can be guarded against, some are so heavy or violent that they cannot be blocked.
While you cannot know for certain if an attack is unblockable until it hits you in the face, it's worth keeping the following in mind:

・Magic, save for Magical Shots, is unblockable.
・Full-body tackles from large creatures cannot be blocked, nor can attacks from above like Downstab or charging attacks.
・Attacks from heavy weapons, like warhammers or massive clubs, are often strong enough to break through one's guard.
・Flame breath or gas attacks will go right through any defense.

If an attack is unblockable, the damage number it generates will be larger than normal. Against such attacks, you have two options: either dodge out of the way completely... or attack your foe before they have a chance to strike.

A few enemies may be armed with tower shields and the like, which will let them guard against nearly any kind of attack. Sadly, Arche isn't quite big enough to use one herself.
Commanding AI Characters
While your AI companions will generally act on their own, there are three ways you can direct their actions: Follow Mode, Magic Usage, and Formation.

"Follow Mode" sets how, or if, AI characters will follow the controlled character. Follow Mode for the entire party can be quickly changed with the F3 key by default. It can also be changed in the main menu under Party Commands>Follow Mode, where you can set each member's follow preferences individually.


Follow Me 		AI characters will follow the player character, and attempt to stay in formation in combat. Useful for staying grouped up to fight an enemy together.

Fight Freely 		AI characters will follow the player when not in combat, but will disregard formation and attack aggressively when faced with monsters. Good for situations when monsters are spread out or you need everyone to be flexible in their positions when attacking.

Stay There 		AI characters will stop in their tracks and remain where they are. If faced with combat, they will engage the enemy and immediately return to their spots when finished. Vital for some puzzles.


"Magic Usage" dictates how magic users should spend their MP. Party-wide Magic Usage settings can be changed by pressing F4 by default. It can also be changed in the main menu under Party Commands>Magic Usage, where you can set each member's usage preferences individually.


Don't use magic 		Forbids use of all magic that would consume MP; mages still use their non-MP elemental attacks.

Heal only 		Mages will only use their healing spells.

Be conservative 		Mages will only occasionally use attack magic, will heal normally, and will not use support magic.

Normal use 		Mages will use healing, attack and support magic as appropriate.

Cast like mad 		Your mages will attempt to burn through their MP as fast as possible, chain-casting attack spells and keeping support spells up as much as possible.


At the start of combat, your party members will take position as dictated by the formation set in the Formation menu.

Formation can be set from the menu, under Orders>Formation. Formation only has an effect when your Follow Mode is set to Follow Me.
Scroll Mode
Pressing F6 (by default) will allow you to change how the screen scrolls.
Pressing F6 will cycle through the Scroll Modes in this order:


Adjustible Mode 		The default mode, with the camera centered on the controlled character. The camera can be temporarily shifted using the following keys:
→ Home key: Scroll screen left.
→ End key: Scroll screen right.
→ PageUp key: Scroll screen up.
→ PageDown key: Scroll screen down.

Lock-On Mode 		The camera will scroll to keep both the player character and the player's current target on screen as best it can. Good for bosses and high-mobility enemies.

Long-Range Mode 		The camera will pan in such a way as to give the player character a wide view of what is in front of them. Good for setting up long-range attacks.
